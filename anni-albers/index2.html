Designing as Visual Organization

Excerpts from Chapter 10 of the book On Weaving, by Anni Albers, 1965

About the Author

Anni Albers was born in Berlin, Germany in 1899 as Annelise Elsa Fleischmann. Her father ran a furniture manufacturing business and her mother came from the family that ran the largest publisher in Germany, Ullstein Verlag. She was encouraged from an early age by her family for her artistic interests. Anni married Josef Albers in 1925 three after he helped her apply a second time for the art school Bauhuas, which she would later teach at.

She was at the forefront of modern textile design and was the first textile artist to have a solo exhibition at the MoMA. Anni is considered the most important textile artist of the 20th century. She died in 1994 in Connecticut.
